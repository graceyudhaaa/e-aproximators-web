How does it works?

The algorithm that use is that from 18th putnam 1958, the question on the original exam is to prove that is true. This is the original question

"A sequence of numbers αi ∈ [0, 1] is chosen at random. Show that the expected value of n, where ∑1n αi > 1, ∑1n-1 αi ≤ 1 is e."

You can read the mathematical solution here

We choose a random number beetwen 0 and 1 exclusive and another random number and add the sum, if the sum hasn't exceed 1, we continue to generate random number and sum it to the total until it exceed 1. if we do this in infinite amount of time and we count how many time we get into one and average it, the answer will be e.

Because it generated from random number, the approximate value of e could be different although the number of itteration stay the same. My explaination might confuse you, fortunately Major Prep has a video explaining it, in fact, the inspiration for this web was from his video
